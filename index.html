Lessons tailored to you

Most requested

Complete Beginners

Never touched a car before. We start in quiet residential streets before anything busier, at whatever pace actually works for you.

A first lesson usually starts with the basics — mirrors, seat position, controls — before moving off on a quiet residential road nearby. No motorways, no pressure.

Confidence-first

Nervous Drivers

If a previous instructor rushed you, or driving just makes you anxious, lessons here are slower and more patient by design.

We'll usually talk through what's making you anxious before even starting the engine, then build up gradually — busier roads only once you're ready for them.

Already passed

Refresher Lessons

Passed a while ago and lost the habit? A few refresher lessons before you're back on the road solo, motorway included if you want it.

We start with a quick assessment of where you're at, then spend the lesson on whatever's actually gone rusty — no need to relearn things you've already got.

No clutch

Automatic Lessons

No clutch, no stalling — a shorter learning curve for a lot of people, and the transmission most new UK drivers now pass in.

With no clutch to manage, lessons can focus earlier on road positioning, junctions, and hazard awareness instead of gear control.

Full control

Manual Lessons

Full control of any car, not just automatics. We make the clutch feel natural from lesson one, not lesson five.

Expect extra time in the first lesson or two on clutch control and biting point — usually in a quiet car park or dead-end road before joining traffic.

04 — Automatic or manual

Not sure which one? Pick one.

Automatic

  • No clutch or gear changes to think about
  • Often a shorter route to test-ready
  • Good if driving itself makes you anxious
  • You can only then drive automatic cars

Manual

  • Licence covers both manual and automatic cars
  • Full control over gears and engine braking
  • Takes a little longer to feel natural
  • Still the majority of used cars on UK roads
